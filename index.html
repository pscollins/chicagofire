Tournament Rules

What is the Great Chicago Fire?

The Great Chicago Fire is the University of Chicago's mock trial tournament. As of 2015, it is the only collegiate mock trial tournament hosted in the city of Chicago. The Great Chicago Fire is unique among mock trial tournaments because it features bracket-style elimination.

Are teams sent home early?

When a school loses a round, they drop into a normal tournament with weighted pairings, like a standard AMTA style tournament, so every team will play a full eight rounds. The champion of the regular tournament will be awarded 3rd place.

What is bracket style elimination?

Mockers will compete as a school, instead of as independent teams. There will be sixteen schools at the tournament; each school will have two teams. First round, each school will be paired with another school. For example, if School 1 was paired with School 2, each of School 1â€™s teams (1A and 1B) would the corresponding team from School 2 (2A and 2B, respectively). For each pairing of schools, there are two trials, each with three ballots, for a total of six ballots (1A v.1B & 2A v. 2B, three ballots each). From these six ballots, one of the schools is determined the winner of the pairing, and the other school is eliminated, and moves on to the next round where they compete against another school that won in the first round, until only two schools are left in the bracket.
